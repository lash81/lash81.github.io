About

Hello, I'm Lauren Shum! I'm currently a junior in Electrical & Computer Engineering at Duke University, where I research thin-film organic photovoltaics and lead the Duke Smart Home, a live-in laboratory that facilitates skill-development for students interested in creating technology for improving quality of life. In the past, I've been a Product Development intern for LP Amina, an energy engineering company in Beijing seeking to purge pollution from coal combustion. While there, I developed process parameter calculation tools, picked up heat-exchange-related Mandarin on the fly, and hiked all over China (the picture up top is from Wutai Shan, Shanxi Province).

I'm extremely interested in energy and environmental issues, software, hardware, and using the latter two to address the former. If we have anything in common, I'd love to hear from you!
